Tone Wheel

A scale is a subset of the cycle of 12 notes from C to B. In particular, scales are considered equivalent if they are rotations of each other-- i.e. a major scale where each note is increased by one note is still considered a major scale, just in a different key. Many scales were found to be interesting to musicians, and given particular names. Click on a button to see that scale represented on the chromatic circle. Drag the wheel around to view that scale in different keys. Blacked out notes are not present in the scale. The yellow note represents the root (initial) note of the scale. Press the play button to play the scale.

Modes are built off of assigning different root notes to the same underlying scale. On this page, the mode buttons are initially arranged in chromatic order, from the root being assigned the first note of a scale, to the seventh. Click the "swap" icon with the blue arrows to switch between the chromatic circle, which displays the notes in order, to the circle of fifths, which staggers the notes in a pattern of fifths, swapping every other note on the chromatic circle with its opposite. This will also reorder the mode buttons in the same fashion, incidentally arranging them from "brightest" (most sharps) to "darkest" (most flats) (why does this happen?). Patterns begin to reveal themselves on the tone wheel -- for instance, we see the major scale consists only of consecutive notes on the circle of fifths. The harmonic minor takes one note from this pattern and moves it several degrees around the circle. The melodic minor takes two. What else can you discover?
